📋 Session Framework

⏱ Phase 1 — Set-up & Activation (0–3 min) ▼
  • 1State the topic clearly. Read the title from the guideline/QRH.
  • 2Ask: "What do you already know about this?" — activate prior knowledge (1 min).
  • 3Set the scene with a 1–2 sentence clinical trigger relevant to the topic.
  • 4Confirm the primary resource to be used.
⏱ Phase 2 — Core Teaching (3–20 min) ▼
  • 5Walk through the guideline/algorithm step by step. Learner follows on their own copy.
  • 6Pause at decision points — ask the learner to predict the next step before revealing it.
  • 7Highlight the top 3 most important / most commonly missed points.
  • 8Cover doses, thresholds, and time-critical actions explicitly.
  • 9Link to relevant pharmacology, physiology, or anatomy — ask rather than tell.
  • 10For procedural topics: cover patient position, probe placement, key landmarks, needle trajectory.
⏱ Phase 3 — Consolidation & FRCA Links (20–27 min) ▼
  • 11Ask the learner to generate 5 key learning points themselves.
  • 12Ask 1–2 FRCA-style questions (MCQ or structured oral format).
  • 13Identify gaps: "What was new? What would you look up before seeing this case?"
  • 14Signpost a further reading resource (guideline URL, BJA Education, tutorial).
⏱ Phase 4 — Log & Handover (27–30 min) ▼
  • 15Record the session in the Excel Tracking Sheet.
  • 16Nominate the next presenter — they scan the QR code for their topic.
  • 17Optional: submit a brief ePortfolio reflection.
